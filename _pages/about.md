Hello! I'm Min-Geun Park, currently a Ph.D. student in Mechanical Engineering at The University of Texas at Austin, where I am a member of the [Human-Enabled Robotic Technology(HERO Lab)](https://sites.utexas.edu/herolab/) under the supervision of [Dr. Ann Fey](https://www.me.utexas.edu/people/faculty-directory/amfey).

I received my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering, specializing in Mechatronics and Control Systems, from the [Advanced Intelligent Mechatronics Research Laboratory (AIMRL)](https://aimrl.gatech.edu/) at the Georgia Institute of Technology, where I also completed my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering.

My research focuses on bimanual robotic systems, with an emphasis on teleoperation, shared autonomy, and human–robot interaction. I am particularly interested in real-time control, motion generation, and the integration of haptic feedback for coordinated manipulation.

Previously, I worked on the [Magnetic Leadscrew (ML)](https://scholar.google.com/citations?view_op=view_citation&hl=en&user=BtnYdO4AAAAJ&citation_for_view=BtnYdO4AAAAJ:d1gkVwhDpl0C) project under the guidance of [Dr. Kok-Meng Lee](https://aimrl.gatech.edu/kmlee.html) and mentorship of [Dr. Wenjing Li](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=aZCe2VQAAAAJ), where I explored intelligent mechatronic systems and nonlinear dynamics.

Beyond academia, I enjoy developing mobile applications, as well as surfing and snowboarding.
